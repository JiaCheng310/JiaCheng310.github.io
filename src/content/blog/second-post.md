When a blog feels hard to read, the problem is usually not one dramatic mistake. It is the accumulation of tiny frictions: wide lines, weak contrast, loud accents, and unclear section breaks.

## A few defaults I trust

I like medium line length, quiet colors, and clear date metadata. Readers scan before they commit, so the archive page should feel like a clean index rather than a wall of cards.

## On restraint

Restraint is a feature. If a layout can express the same structure with fewer visual decisions, it usually ages better.
